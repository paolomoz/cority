Take Your Corporate Safety Culture to the Next Level with Data Analytics

Join subject matter experts from Cority and ERM as they discuss safety management topics, culture, and data analytics.

style
navy

What do we mean by safety culture and data analytics, how do we develop a great one, and how do we measure it? Join subject matter experts from Cority and ERM as they discuss safety management and much more.

This webinar is hosted by Ian Cohen from Cority and Caleb Wall from ERM. It will cover:

  • Understanding the terminology of workplace safety culture
  • Analytics tools in use today across the industry
  • Safety management for the 21st century
  • Case studies
  • The power of comprehensive data

Understanding workplace safety culture begins with mastering the terminology that defines it. In this webinar, we will demystify the language of safety culture, providing participants with a clear and concise understanding of essential terms and concepts. This foundational knowledge will serve as a springboard for deeper discussions and insights into fostering a positive, proactive, and resilient safety culture within your organization.

Analytics tools have become indispensable assets in modern safety management, enabling organizations to collect, analyze, and leverage data effectively to identify trends, monitor performance, and make informed decisions. We will explore the latest analytics tools in use across the industry, highlighting their capabilities, benefits, and potential applications in enhancing workplace safety.

Safety management for the 21st century demands a holistic approach that goes beyond compliance and embraces innovation, collaboration, and continuous improvement. This webinar will delve into the principles and practices that define modern safety management, offering actionable strategies and insights to help organizations adapt, evolve, and thrive in today’s dynamic and complex environment.

Case studies will be a pivotal part of this webinar, showcasing real-world examples of organizations that have successfully transformed their safety cultures, implemented innovative solutions, and achieved measurable improvements in safety performance. These case studies will provide valuable lessons, best practices, and inspiration for organizations looking to elevate their safety management practices.

Lastly, we will explore the power of comprehensive data in driving safety excellence. By aggregating and analyzing data from various sources, organizations can gain deeper insights into their safety performance, identify areas for improvement, and develop targeted interventions to enhance workplace safety.
