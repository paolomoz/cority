Unlocking Insights with an Online Process

By taking this process online, buyers can bring to life what would otherwise be stored away. An online approach opens up the possibility of querying all the information collected from suppliers, enabling a deeper understanding of what is happening across the supply chain. For instance, a company might face specific risks related to human rights. If a supplier does not have a human rights policy, this becomes a business risk.

With a manual process, comparing such responses across suppliers is challenging. An online system, however, makes it easier to track and compare key risks like forced labor or child labor in high-risk regions. By integrating ESG metrics into this online process, companies can better assess their suppliers’ compliance with sustainability standards.

For instance, a company might face specific risks related to human rights practices. If a supplier does not have a robust human rights policy, it becomes a business risk that could impact the buyer’s ESG performance or even lead to reputational damage. With a manual process, identifying and comparing such responses across suppliers is time-consuming and often unreliable. An online system, however, streamlines this process by allowing buyers to quickly assess compliance on key issues, such as forced labor or child labor in high-risk regions.

By integrating ESG metrics into this online process, companies can more effectively measure supplier performance against sustainability standards, track progress, and ensure they are meeting their reporting and compliance requirements. The ability to instantly cross-reference suppliers’ ESG data enables faster and more informed decision-making, further enhancing supply chain transparency.
