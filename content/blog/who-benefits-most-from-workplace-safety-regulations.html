Workplace safety regulations have fast become a cornerstone of several industries; put in place to protect several parties, communities surrounding the industry, and so much more. This blog will review what these safety regulations are, who benefits the most from them, and examples of employee vs employer benefits.

What are workplace safety regulations?

These safety regulations are put in place to ensure the workplace is a safe environment for those in and adjacent to it. These are more prominent in high-risk industries, or workplaces that require physical labor, though office buildings will have them as well.

The vast majority of developed nations have workplace safety regulations. A few examples are OSHA in the US, Canada’s Occupational Health and Safety Regulations (SOR/86-304), or the UK’s Workplace (Health, Safety and Welfare) Regulations. A great example of workplace safety regulations that received significant exposure in recent years is the required use of Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) in spaces such as healthcare facilities.

Who benefits most from workplace safety regulations?

The very short answer to this question is “lots of people”. However, it’s appropriate to split that group into two distinct camps: employers and employees. Here’s a breakdown of the benefits for these two groups – as well as smaller sub-sections within them.

Employees

Reducing the risk of injury or illness:

An obvious benefit to workers when asking the question “who benefits most from workplace safety regulations?” would be an overall safer workplace, resulting in a reduced risk to employees. To put things into perspective, since the UK’s HSWA regulations were put in place in 1974, workplace fatalities have reduced by nearly 80% year-over-year.

Final Thoughts

Although this blog divided the benefits of safety regulations into employer and employee groups, it’s clear that these regulations really benefit everyone. Safety regulations play a crucial role in fostering a safe and productive environment for both employees and employers. By minimizing risks of injury and illness, improving well-being, and promoting job satisfaction, these regulations benefit workers significantly. Employers, on the other hand, enjoy reduced costs, improved productivity, and a better reputation, all while fostering employee retention and legal protection.

Ultimately, prioritizing safety creates a win-win situation for everyone involved, ensuring that workplaces remain not only compliant but also thriving in the long term. Therefore, the questions organizations should be asking are, “how can we maximize the benefits of workplace safety regulations?” and “how can we ensure our workforce feels involved in the refinement and application of these regulations?”
