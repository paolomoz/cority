The use of data-driven EHS compliance software is of critical importance when it comes to the management of risks attached to the environment, health and safety (EHS). Failure to comply with EHS regulations that are usually strict and demanding can have devastating consequences for your business. Your brand may suffer irreparable damage in the public domain. Even worse, you may have to close your business and may have to serve jail time for negligence in this regard. It is imperative not to neglect issues relating to environment, health and safety as it affects so much in terms of what’s happening in the world and around us. EHS compliance software allows you to ensure regulatory adherence and avoid disastrous failures.

EHS Risk Management Software and the Environment

EHS risk management software can help your business to manage risks relating to the environment – and most importantly – to comply with environmental law that aims to regulate or manage the impact of human activity on the natural environment. Think animals, plants and other living and non-living things that are not artificial and depend on the Earth for survival. Think natural resources such as sunlight, water, minerals, forests, atmosphere, land, fisheries and more.

The risks of triggering man-made environmental disasters (e.g. oil spills, radiation leaks, severe air pollution, uncontrolled fires, toxic waste spill, toxic gas leaks, etc.) are real. It is best to make use of compliance management software in order to make sure that your business practices meet the strict standards set by environmental law.

EHS Compliance Software and Health and Safety

EHS compliance software should be a critical component of any strategy to identify and rectify potential health and safety hazards in both the public and private spheres. Beyond helping with compliance when it comes to health and safety regulations, this data-driven software platform can help to save lives and at a minimum help to keep employees happy.

Happy employees are after all loyal, productive members of society who appreciate the proactive steps being taken to ensure their health and safety at work. This also has a ripple effect that impacts their personal lives beyond their places of work, the lives of their loved ones and other people positively. It helps to provide the building blocks for healthy communities and in the final equation healthy nations.

It should be clear that EHS compliance software is not merely a nice-to-have, but of critical importance. Gone are the days when businesses and organizations could do as they please and destroy the lives of many people in the process. This while destroying the environment, leaving a legacy of bad actions backed by bad decisions behind that are still negatively affecting people today.

If you are not convinced, go speak to the people of Love Canal in Niagara Falls, New York – those who are still alive to tell the story about what happened there. Alternatively, ask those who can remember the Great Smog of 1952, the Chernobyl disaster, the Three Mile Island accident, the various disastrous oil spills (Exxon Valdez oil spill, Deepwater Horizon oil spill, etc.) and many other man-made disasters that happened – disasters that could have been avoided by being proactive instead of reactive.
