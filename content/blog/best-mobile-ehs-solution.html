Choosing the right mobile EHS solution will transform how your organization captures field data, responds to incidents, and drives safety performance.

With the global EHS software market projected to reach $4.5 billion by 2029, more organizations are replacing paper-based processes and static desktop tools with mobile-first platforms.

But not all mobile EHS solutions deliver the same value.

The difference between a platform that drives adoption and one that sits unused often comes down to six core capabilities.

Here’s what to look for when evaluating mobile EHS software for your organization. 

1. Works on Any Device With No New Hardware Needed

A mobile-first solution should run on smartphones, tablets, and desktops without requiring device upgrades or app store downloads. Using existing hardware keeps deployment simple and reduces rollout costs.

2. Easy and Intuitive for Employees to Use

To get reliable data from the field, the user interface must be simple, familiar, and consistent across all devices. Good mobile design reduces training time, increases adoption, and encourages more timely reporting. 

3. Limited Maintenance for Your IT Team

Native apps often come with hidden complexity: constant OS updates, retesting, re-approvals, and device standardization. Mobile-first, browser-based solutions minimize the burden on IT teams by eliminating app-store dependencies and keeping everyone on the same version automatically.

4. Real-Time Visibility for Better Decision-Making

Mobile tools give leaders instant insight into what’s happening across sites. Field data automatically feeds dashboards and reporting, supporting faster cycle times, improved task closure, and more consistent compliance, especially when replacing paper or Excel-based processes. 

“With Cority… we’re able to capture high-potential events, investigate them, train employees and make quick adjustments to reduce the probability of it happening in the future.”  

– Robert Wesley, HSE Director, TGS Nopec 

5. Supports All Key EHS Workflows Online or Offline

A strong mobile platform should offer broad functionality across the full range of EHS programs, including:

  • Incident reporting & investigation 
  • Audits & Inspections 
  • Change management
  • Industrial hygiene sampling
  • Permits to work 
  • Medical visits & records
  • Corrective actions and tasks
  • Surveys and checklists
  • Mobile dashboards

It also needs to work reliably without connectivity, syncing data automatically once the device is back online.
