Each year in the United States, approximately 2.8 million workers suffer a job-related injury or illness.1 Beyond the pain and suffering these occupational incidents cause, they exert a considerable influence on overall business productivity and profitability. In 2023 alone, U.S. companies incurred over $58 billion in costs linked to disabling workplace incidents, driving up their workers’ compensation insurance expenses, and creating a substantial financial burden for these organizations to overcome.2

Onsite occupational health (OH) clinics play a crucial role in mitigating these costs. However, realizing better health outcomes is more than simply making OH services more readily available to your workforce. Onsite occupational health clinics must be optimized with best practice protocols and workflows to be truly effective at reducing the risks of on-the-job injuries and illnesses. Furthermore, access to enterprise-grade OH software can be critical in building an occupational health practice that delivers real results.

  • What onsite occupational health clinics are and how it saves organizations money;

Understanding the Role of an Onsite Occupational Health Clinics

An onsite OH clinic is a specialized healthcare facility within or near a workplace. Its purpose is to address employees’ specific health risks and wellness needs while ensuring compliance with legal standards. These clinics offer various services to promote, maintain, and restore workers’ health and wellbeing. Key areas managed by onsite OH clinics typically include:

Preplacement services:

Services include conducting pre-employment health evaluations to ensure job suitability, performing fitness-for-duty assessments to verify employees’ capability to safely perform job tasks, and providing guidance on reasonable workplace health accommodations.

Services include conducting pre-employment health evaluations to ensure job suitability, performing fitness-for-duty assessments to verify employees’ capability to safely perform job tasks, and providing guidance on reasonable workplace health accommodations.

Preventive care:

Services include conducting health screenings for indicators like blood pressure and cholesterol levels, administer routine vaccinations and immunizations, and perform health risk assessments to identify potential health concerns proactively.

Services include conducting health screenings for indicators like blood pressure and cholesterol levels, administer routine vaccinations and immunizations, and perform health risk assessments to identify potential health concerns proactively.

Primary care:

Services include offering diagnosis and treatment for common work-related illnesses and minor injuries, providing prescription services, overseeing medication management, along with overseeing return-to-work processes.

Services include offering diagnosis and treatment for common work-related illnesses and minor injuries, providing prescription services, overseeing medication management, along with overseeing return-to-work processes.

Wellness programs:

Services include offering nutrition and weight management counseling to promote healthy lifestyles, smoking cessation programs, stress management resources, and mental health supports.

Services include offering nutrition and weight management counseling to promote healthy lifestyles, smoking cessation programs, stress management resources, and mental health supports.

Onsite OH clinics help reduce the frequency and severity of workplace injuries and illnesses, which in turn lowers healthcare and workers’ compensation costs.

How Onsite Occupational Health Clinics Save Organizations Money

The integration of onsite OH clinicians into the workplace allows them to provide timely and effective care. This ensures that employees have convenient access to medical care with options for walk-in, same-day, and virtual appointments. This convenience reduces the time employees spend away from work, thereby decreasing costs associated with unscheduled absences.

Consider this: Many onsite clinics report a $4 return on investment for every $1 spent on onsite care, with some clinics seeing a 63% reduction in workplace injuries.3 Additionally, companies with onsite clinics often see significant benefits: 64% report reduced medical expenses, nearly 70% observe decreased time lost, and 63% experience fewer emergency room visits.4

Moreover, onsite health clinics can significantly enhance employee productivity and overall wellness. Disengaged employees, due to decreased productivity, cost the U.S. economy between $438 billion and $605 billion annually.5 Access to health services, including onsite health clinics, can help mitigate this issue, leading to a more engaged and productive workforce.

Challenges With Managing Onsite OH Clinics

When organizations establish an onsite OH clinic, developing best practice protocols and workflows is crucial to enhance employee health, mitigate risks, ensure regulatory compliance, and promote overall wellbeing. But health clinics regularly face challenges that hinder their overall effectiveness, impacting health outcomes and organizational compliance.

Here are five critical challenges organizations need to keep in mind when building their onsite OH clinics:

1) Inefficient record-keeping:

Manual handling of employee health medical records can lead to errors, lost documents, and difficulty retrieving employee health histories. This inefficiency not only jeopardizes the accuracy and accessibility of crucial health information, but also hampers the quality of care provided while increasing the risk of compromised health data privacy.

2) Excessive administrative burden:

Manual appointment management processes, for example, increase OH staff’s workload, diverting their focus from more critical tasks. By relying on manual booking processes, managing appointments is often chaotic, resulting in overbookings, missed appointments, and longer wait times for employees, reducing the patient experience and impacting return visits.

Manual compilation of health data for reporting purposes is cumbersome, time consuming and prone to errors, ultimately impacting the accuracy of reports, and downstream decision-making.

1) Pick a solution that will grow with you:

As a result, firms should prioritize software solutions that can be easily configured to meet diverse business needs, while offering capabilities to grow and adapt as conditions and business requirements change. Opt for a multi-tenant software solution over a single-tenant one, as multi-tenancy is more cost-effective in the long run. Unlike single-tenant software, where you’re stuck with the purchased version and must pay for updates, multi-tenant offers a lower cost of ownership and ensures you always have access to the latest, highest-quality version of the tool without extra costs for updates.
